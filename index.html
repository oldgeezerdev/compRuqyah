Key Steps to Take Before Seeking a Spiritual Cure (Ruqyah)

Correct Your Core Beliefs

  • Focus on a correct and sincere belief in Allah (swt).
  • Your inner faith is the foundation that guides your actions.
  • It is the most powerful tool, as shown by the prayers of the Prophets in distress.

Sincerely Repent

Turn back to Allah (swt) and sincerely apologize for your mistakes. True repentance means:

  • Feeling genuine regret.
  • Asking for forgiveness.
  • Making a firm decision not to repeat the sin.
  • Making things right with anyone you have wronged.

Remove Sources of Sin

Clean up your environment. Get rid of anything that leads you to disobey Allah (swt), such as:

  • Forbidden music and videos.
  • Bad influences and friendships.
  • Any objects or habits related to superstition or innovation in religion.

Increase Your Good Deeds

Actively do more good. This includes:

  • Praying on time.
  • Reading the Quran.
  • Giving to charity.
  • Performing voluntary fasts and night prayers.

Good deeds have the power to wipe away misdeeds.

Pray Sincerely from the Heart

  • Constantly ask Allah (swt) for help.
  • Pour your heart out to Him, especially during times when prayers are most likely to be accepted, like late at night or while fasting.

Have Positive Thoughts About Allah (swt)

  • Always be confident in Allah (swt)'s mercy and power.
  • Believe with certainty that He can and will relieve your distress.
  • Your outlook has a powerful effect on the outcome.

Constantly Seek Forgiveness

  • Make a habit of asking Allah (swt) for forgiveness.
  • When you consistently do this, Allah (swt) promises relief from every difficulty and a way out from every hardship.

How to Perform a Self-Ruqyah

  • Make your ablution (wudu).
  • Find a clean and quiet place.
  • Have the sincere intention to seek healing from Allah (swt) alone.

قُلْ أُوحِيَ إِلَيَّ أَنَّهُ اسْتَمَعَ نَفَرٌ مِّنَ الْجِنِّ فَقَالُوا إِنَّا سَمِعْنَا قُرْآنًا عَجَبًا ﴿١﴾ يَهْدِي إِلَى الرُّشْدِ فَآمَنَّا بِهِ ۖ وَلَن نُّشْرِكَ بِرِبِّنَا أَحَدًا ﴿٢﴾ وَأَنَّهُ تَعَالَىٰ جَدُّ رَبِّنَا مَا اتَّخَذَ صَاحِبَةً وَلَا وَلَدًا ﴿٣﴾ وَأَنَّهُ كَانَ يَقُولُ سَفِيهُنَا عَلَى اللَّهِ شَطَطًا ﴿٤﴾ وَأَنَّا ظَنَنَّا أَن لَّن تَقُولَ الْإِنْسُ وَالْجِنُّ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا ﴿۵﴾ وَأَنَّهُ كَانَ رِجَالٌ مِّنَ الْإِنْسِ يَعُوذُونَ بِرِجَالٍ مِّنَ الْجِنِّ فَزَادُوهُمْ رَهَقًا ﴿٦﴾ وَأَنَّهُمْ ظَنُّوا كَمَا ظَنَنتُمْ أَن لَّن يَبْعَثَ اللَّهُ أَحَدًا ﴿٧﴾ وَأَنَّا لَمَسْنَا السَّمَاءَ فَوَجَدْنَاهَا مُلِئَتْ حَرَسًا شَدِيدًا وَشُهُبًا ﴿٨﴾ وَأَنَّا كُنَّا نَقْعُدُ مِنْهَا مَقَاعِدَ لِلسَّمْعِ ۖ فَمَن يَسْتَمِعِ الْآنَ يَجِدْ لَهُ شِهَابًا رَّصَدًا ﴿٩﴾
Say, It has been revealed to me that a group of jinn listened and said, 'Indeed, we have heard an amazing Qur'an. [1] It guides to the right course, and we have believed in it. And we will never associate with our Lord anyone. [2] And that exalted is the nobleness of our Lord; He has not taken a wife or a son. [3] And that our foolish one has been saying about Allah an excessive transgression. [4] And we had thought that mankind and the jinn would never speak about Allah a lie. [5] And there were men from mankind who sought refuge in men from the jinn, so they increased them in burden. [6] And they had thought, as you thought, that Allah would never send anyone. [7] And we have sought to reach the heaven but found it filled with powerful guards and burning flames. [8] And we used to sit therein in positions for hearing, but whoever listens now will find a burning flame lying in wait for him. [9]

Shifa - General Shifā' (Healing & Relief)

Surah At-Tawbah (9:14)
قَاتِلُوهُمْ يُعَذِّبْهُمُ اللَّهُ بِأَيْدِيكُمْ وَيُخْزِهِمْ وَيَنصُرْكُمْ عَلَيْهِمْ وَيَشْفِ صُدُورَ قَوْمٍ مُّؤْمِنِينَ
Fight them; Allah will punish them by your hands and will disgrace them and give you victory over them and satisfy the breasts of a believing people
Surah Al-Naḥل (16:69)
ثُمَّ كُلِي مِن كُلِّ الثَّمَرَاتِ فَاسْلُكِي سُبُلَ رَبِّكِ ذُلُلًا ۚ يَخْرُجُ مِن بُطُونِهَا شَرَابٌ مُّخْتَلِفٌ أَلْوَانُهُ فِيهِ شِفَاءٌ لِّلنَّاسِ ۗ إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَةً لِّقَوْمٍ يَتَفَكَّرُونَ
Then eat from all the fruits and follow the ways of your Lord laid down [for you]. There emerges from their bellies a drink, varying in colors, in which there is healing for people. Indeed in that is a sign for a people who give thought.
Surah Al-Isrā' (17:82)
وَنُنَزِّلُ مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ مَا هُوَ شِفَاءٌ وَرَحْمَةٌ لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ ۙ وَلَا يَزِيدُ الظَّالِمِينَ إِلَّا خَسَارًا
And We send down of the Qur'an that which is healing and mercy for the believers, but it does not increase the wrongdoers except in loss.
Surah Al-Shu'arā (26:80)
وَإِذَا مَرِضْتُ فَهُوَ يَشْفِينِ
And when I am ill, it is He who cures me
Surah Al-Fuṣṣilat (41:44)
وَلَوْ جَعَلْنَاهُ قُرْآنًا أَعْجَمِيًّا لَّقَالُوا لَوْلَا فُصِّلَتْ آيَاتُهُ ۖ أَأَعْجَمِيٌّ وَعَرَبِيٌّ ۗ قُلْ هُوَ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا هُدًى وَشِفَاءٌ ۖ وَالَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ فِي آذَانِهِمْ وَقْرٌ وَهُوَ عَلَيْهِمْ عَمًى ۚ أُولَٰئِكَ يُنَادَوْنَ مِن مَّكَانٍ بَعِيدٍ
Had We revealed it as a non-Arabic Quran, they would have certainly argued, "If only its verses were made clear [in our language]. What! A non-Arabic revelation for an Arab audience!" Say, [O Prophet,] "It is a guide and a healing to the believers. As for those who disbelieve, there is deafness in their ears and blindness to it [in their hearts]. It is as if they are being called from a faraway place."
